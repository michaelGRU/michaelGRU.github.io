Modeling Nonlinear Dynamics and Mechanical Systems

Chaos, in terms of mathematics and dynamical systems, is the nature of seemingly random states of disorder that are governed by deterministic laws that are highly sensitive to initial conditions.

Take two double pendulums, for instance, if we could duplicate the same initial condition, the two oscillators should, in theory, behave the same as time increases.

However, due to the unknown and uncontrollable nature of our universe, it is impossible to control the two mechanical systems such that they have precisely the same initial conditions. For instance, air resistance is not consistent throughout space. It is not possible to set two pendulums at the exact same height due to human error.

As we can observe in a dynamical mechanical system like a double pendulum, the motion of the two pendulums with seemingly matching initial conditions would diverge as time goes on. After some initial time t, the movement becomes chaotic and unpredictable, rendering long-term prediction of a deterministic system seemingly impossible, which leads to the question: can we ever observe all variables within a system to precisely predict the future?
